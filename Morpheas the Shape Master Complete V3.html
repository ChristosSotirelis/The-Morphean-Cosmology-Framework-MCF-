Why This Exists

No current system aboard any space station combines adaptive body‑conformal rest with programmable axial skeletal loading. Morpheas solves both in one rigid‑shell or free‑floating sack architecture with a breathable mesh and intelligent pneumatic matrix. ISS treadmill exercise generates ground reaction forces 75–77% below Earth walking — insufficient to prevent bone loss. Current sleeping bags do not support neutral body posture, causing pressure points and CO₂ pooling. Morpheas is the first integrated solution for rest, passive bone loading, active training, and medical monitoring.

System A — Adaptive Comfort Shell (Hoodie‑Sack)

Rigid outer frame (or free‑floating military canvas) + 3D mesh inner liner + full‑body hexagonal inflatable matrix. The design integrates ergonomic head opening, hoodie, dynamic arm slots, and dedicated neck support.

Ergonomic features

Head opening + Hoodie

The sack does not enclose the face. A dedicated jacket‑style split at the head region allows the astronaut’s head to remain completely outside the enclosure. A soft hoodie structure (matching outer canvas) gently frames the top and back of the head, providing light containment and comfort. No transparent window needed – direct visibility and communication.

Neck support pod integrated behind the neck (not skull) to relax cervical muscles and enable proper spinal stretching.

Dynamic Arm Slots

Longitudinal openings from shoulder to lower torso. Velcro strap at elbow locks arm inside during sleep; released for activity. Inflatable lips on slot edges can seal around the arm (inflated) or deflate to allow easy exit. Provides both security and freedom.

All access points support instant emergency release (mechanical pull‑cord & full deflation). The system never traps the user.

Dual‑Mode Pneumatic Loading (A1 + A2)

The hexagonal matrix is divided into two independently controlled pneumatic subsystems, enabling both conformal comfort and true axial gravity simulation.

System A1 · Conformal Comfort

Target zones: Torso, arms, legs (non‑axial regions)
Pressure: Low (10–20 kPa)
Function: Eliminates drift anxiety, maintains neutral body posture (NBP), provides proprioceptive feedback, enables thermal regulation (slight over‑inflation closes mesh gaps for warmth).

System A2 · Axial Gravity Simulation

Target zones: Hoodie top‑of‑head pod, shoulder yoke, foot boot (soles)
Pressure: Higher (up to 40 kPa)
Function: Creates top‑down / bottom‑up compression → simulates Earth gravity (1g = 9.81 m/s²). Protects spine, prevents bone loss, provides “grounded” sensation.

Force distribution (critical):
Shoulders & feet: 100% reference load.
Top of head: 85% less than shoulders (only 15% load) → protects cranium while maintaining axial tension through spine.

Both systems share the same TPU honeycomb but are isolated via independent valves and pressure sensors. The rigid outer shell (or non‑stretch canvas) provides the mechanical ground that converts internal pressure into net body force.

System B — Mechanical Load Simulator (MLSS, optional)

For active high‑intensity training (Mode 2) and precise inertial mass measurement, segmented sector actuators apply calibrated axial load 30–150 kg equivalent. Closed‑loop PID at 500 Hz, DLC‑coated steel sectors, BLDC servomotors.

MLSS specifications

Force range:294 N – 1472 N (30–150 kg eq)
Control:PID 500 Hz, Kp 0.8, Ki 0.15, Kd 0.05
Resolution:±1 kg
Asymmetry monitor:>5% diff → auto‑equalize

Safety & KERS (optional)

Force exceed 200%:Immediate deactivation
KERS:Motor as generator → feeds station battery
Power loss:Springs return to zero; NC valves hold inflation

For the free‑floating soft sack version, System A2 (pneumatic axial loading) fully replaces MLSS, drastically reducing mass and complexity while retaining gravity simulation.

Biometrics · Thermal Control · Mass Measurement

Sensors (integrated into mesh & pods)

  • ECG (dry electrodes) – heart rate, arrhythmia
  • Respiration – strain‑sensitive fibre band
  • Skin temperature – 8 thermistor points
  • Contact pressure map – piezoresistive film (shoulder/hip)
  • SpO₂ (optional optical clip)
  • CO₂ proximity – NDIR head‑zone sensor

Thermal & wellness

Thermal comfort mode: Slight over‑inflation closes mesh gaps, reduces airflow → retains body heat. No electric heater. Hot air can be circulated through the bladder for rapid warming.

Inertial mass measurement: User fully immobilised by inflated cells → small pulsed axial force (foot boot) → measure displacement vs canvas spring rate → m = F/a → displayed as Earth‑equivalent kilograms (±1 kg accuracy).

Emergency Egress & Safety Architecture

Certified release paths

Electronic: Single button → all NC solenoid valves open → self‑vent through mesh → free in ≤4 seconds.
Mechanical backup (zero electronics): Pull‑cord at collar zone physically unseats burst‑disk valve → full pressure dump in <2 seconds. Operable in darkness, one‑handed, no tools.

Safety thresholds

  • Force >200% target → full deactivation
  • Shoulder asymmetry >5% → auto‑equalize
  • Power loss → NC valves hold inflation, springs return to zero
  • ECG cardiac alarm → automatic load reduction to Mode 0
  • All sensors failed → manual pull‑tab release
