⚡ FUNDAMENTAL SHIELDING MECHANISMS & IMPEDANCE CASCADE THEORY

Attenuation via reflection (high‑frequency, impedance mismatch), absorption (skin‑depth loss), and multiple internal reflections. The impedance mismatch cascade uses alternating conductive (low Z) and magnetic/dielectric (higher Z) layers to force wave re‑reflection, maximizing total shielding factor (SF).

Schelkunoff transmission‑line model: intrinsic impedance Zm = √(jωμ / (σ+jωϵ)). Multilayer stacks with odd number of layers often outperform even‑layer configurations due to outer/interlayer impedance contrast.
Skin depth δ (copper): δ = 66/√f mm → 0.66 mm @ 10 kHz, 2.1 µm @ 1 GHz. Each δ ≈ 8.7 dB absorption.
Reflection dominance: >100 dB from thin Cu above 100 kHz; at low frequencies absorption requires bulk or permeable layers.

🌫️ GAP FUNCTION: VACUUM / LOW‑DENSITY GAS & STRUCTURAL DECOUPLING

Electromagnetic decoupling

Gaps ≥12.7 mm prevent magnetic short‑circuits; outer layer reduces flux density before inner mu‑metal. Vacuum eliminates gas‑phase breakdown and improves dielectric strength. For f >10 kHz, shielding factor rises with gap distance.

Vibration isolation

Vacuum gaps block acoustic transmission. In air gaps, porous absorbers damp structural‑acoustic resonances. Large spans use voxelized metamaterials and quasi‑zero stiffness (QZS) supports to decouple global modes.

🌐 DOME / ELLIPSOID GEOMETRY ADVANTAGES

Spherical shielding factor SF = 9μr / [(2μr+1)(μr+2) − 2(a/b)³(μr−1)²]. Curved surfaces eliminate corner flux concentrations and reduce seam length — dominant leakage path. Quantitatively: 20–30% higher SF vs rectangular box, 10–20 dB less edge leakage.

⛰️ DEEP‑EARTH GROUNDING (50–200 m SPANS)

Rod depth: 9–21 m (30–70 ft) to reach moist stable layers; resistance <10 Ω. Doubling depth reduces resistance ~40% (vs. 10% for diameter increase).
Mesh density: Rectangular copper grid, 3–7 m spacing, with perimeter vertical rods. Multi‑concentric ring grids (MCRG) optimize large footprints.
Geomagnetic induction: Low‑inductance grid dissipates solar‑flare currents; soil replacement with clay reduces resistivity >60%.

📈 IMPEDANCE MISMATCH CASCADE — THEORETICAL & PRACTICAL LIMITS

For N nested spherical shells: SF ≈ (μr/2)N. With μr ~ 100k, N=4 → SF ~ 6×10¹⁹ (theoretical). Practical ceiling set by:

  • Residual magnetization (anhysteretic limit)
  • Aperture/seam leakage — dominates >1 kHz
  • Johnson noise from shield walls (thermal current fluctuations)
  • Stress‑induced permeability degradation

Measured passive systems: SF 10³–10⁵ at sub‑Hz; large multilayer rooms report SF ~10⁵ @0.01 Hz, >10⁸ @ >1 Hz. Combined active+passive reaches ~160 dB (10⁸) at > few Hz.
