Exact Conditions for Stable Earth Orbit

Circular Orbital Velocity (the one speed at which a circular orbit is maintained at radius r)
v = √(GM/r)
Where:
G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg² (universal gravitational constant)
M = 5.972 × 10²⁴ kg (mass of Earth)
r = distance from Earth's centre to the satellite (not altitude — altitude + 6,371 km)

Escape Velocity (minimum speed to leave Earth's gravity entirely from radius r)
v_escape = √(2GM/r) = √2 × v_orbital

Key consequence: escape velocity is always exactly √2 ≈ 1.414 times the circular orbital velocity at any given altitude. GEMINI

An orbit is a delicate equilibrium. Any force that removes kinetic energy causes the orbit to decay inward. Any force that adds kinetic energy above the escape threshold causes the object to leave Earth. Both failure modes are well-documented across the orbital spectrum. GEMINI

Orbital Decay — The Inward Spiral

Mechanism — Atmospheric Drag (Primary Cause of Decay in LEO)

Drag removes kinetic energy from the orbit. This paradoxically accelerates the satellite (lower orbits are faster) while lowering the orbital altitude. The satellite descends into denser atmosphere, drag increases further, the orbit decays faster — an accelerating "death spiral." Below ~80 km, aerodynamic heating destroys the satellite within minutes. GEMINI

During solar maximum, UV radiation heats and expands the upper atmosphere, increasing thermospheric density at a given altitude by up to 10×.
