Abstract & Clinical Rationale

The Morpheas Arterial Restoration System is a modular endovascular intervention designed to physically extract atheromatic plaque, eliminating the need for permanent foreign body implantation (stents). Conventional stenting imposes a finite clinical "expiration date" due to restenosis, in-stent thrombosis, and cumulative metallic burden. This system utilizes a proprietary triple-catheter mechanism: an outer stabilization balloon, a distal fixation envelope, and a middle debridement unit equipped with precision-expandable blades and dual-balloon support. By integrating VetiGel (invented by Joe Landolina) as an obligate hemostatic agent, the system enables safe aspiration of plaque particles and blood while ensuring immediate arterial wall sealing. The result is a fully restored, natural arterial lumen without permanent hardware.

Technical Architecture: Triple-Catheter System

1. Outer Stabilizer

Primary catheter expands a high-compliance balloon to anchor the system inside the vessel, providing a rigid working channel and preventing migration during debridement.

2. Distal Envelope (Fixation Catheter)

Passes entirely through the plaque and expands distally. This creates a protective catchment zone that prevents embolic debris from travelling downstream. A dedicated injection port on the backside allows delivery of VetiGel or autologous blood.

3. Middle Debridement Unit

Contains two integrated balloons: a soft support balloon and a precision balloon that expands micro-blades. These blades shave the atheromatic plaque with controlled radial force, preserving the healthy media and adventitia.

Obligate Hemostatic Agent: VetiGel

Inventor: Joe Landolina (Cresilon / Suneris)
Plant-derived polysaccharide gel that mimics the extracellular matrix. Upon contact with bleeding tissue, it self-assembles into a physical mesh, stopping arterial bleeding within seconds and promoting fibrin production. The Morpheas system injects VetiGel through the distal envelope cavity immediately after plaque debridement, then activates main lumen aspiration to remove debris, excess gel, and blood, leaving a clean and sealed arterial wall.

Injection & Suction protocol
- Step A: Deliver VetiGel (or patient blood) via backside injection point.
- Step B: Main cavity vacuum aspirates plaque fragments, old blood, and residual agent.
- Step C: Retraction of all catheters – no foreign body remains.

Procedural workflow (Phase-by-Phase)

  • Primary entry & outer balloon expansion – system stabilization.
  • Distal envelope crosses the lesion, expands to form a distal seal and embolic shield.
  • Injection of VetiGel into the working zone (back cavity).
  • Middle unit deploys micro-blades (via second precision balloon) – active plaque debridement.
  • Simultaneous suction through main cavity removes debris and hemostatic agent.
  • Arterial wall remains sealed by VetiGel – no thrombosis or late restenosis risk.
  • Complete retraction of all catheters; native artery restored without stents.

Clinical advantage: Eliminates the cumulative burden of stents, reduces dual antiplatelet therapy dependency, and removes the patient’s "expiration date" associated with multiple reinterventions.
